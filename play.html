A Woman of No Importance A Play

The Drawing-room at Hunstanton Chase

Allonby

What a comfort it is to have got rid of the men for a little!

lady stufield

Yes; men persecute us dreadfully, don’t they?

Allonby

Pesecute us? I wish they did.

lady Hunstanton

my dear!

Allonby

The annoying thing is that the wretches can be perfect happy without us. That is why I think it is every woman’s duty never to leave them alone for a single moment, except during this short breathin space after dinner; without which I believe we poor women would be absolutely worn to shadows

[_Enter Servants with coffee_.]

lady Hunstaton

Worn to shadows, dear?

Allonby

Yes, Lady Hunstanton. It is such a strain keeping men upto the mark. They are always trying to escape from us.

lady stutfield

It seems to me that it is we who are always trying to escape from them. Men are so very, very heartless. They know their power and use it

[_Takes coffee from Servant_.]

laddy Caroline

What stuff and nonsense all this about men is! The thing to do is to keep men in their properplace.
